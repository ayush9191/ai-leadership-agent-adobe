Adobe Inc. — Q2 FY2024 Earnings Summary
Quarter Ended: May 31, 2024
(Based on Adobe's publicly released Q2 FY2024 earnings press release)

==========================================================
Q2 FY2024 FINANCIAL RESULTS
==========================================================

Revenue: $5.31 billion (up 10% YoY from $4.82 billion in Q2 FY2023)
  - Digital Media segment revenue: $3.91 billion (up 11% YoY)
  - Digital Experience segment revenue: $1.33 billion (up 9% YoY)
  - Publishing and Advertising: $0.07 billion

GAAP Operating Income: $1.89 billion
Non-GAAP Operating Income: $2.55 billion
GAAP Net Income: $1.57 billion
Non-GAAP Net Income: $2.02 billion
GAAP Diluted EPS: $3.49
Non-GAAP Diluted EPS: $4.48

==========================================================
DIGITAL MEDIA — Q2 PERFORMANCE
==========================================================

Digital Media segment revenue: $3.91 billion (up 11% year-over-year)
Digital Media net new ARR: $487 million in Q2 FY2024

Creative Cloud:
  - Creative Cloud ARR: $12.86 billion (end of Q2 FY2024)
  - Adobe Firefly cumulative generations surpassed 9 billion as of Q2 end
  - Generative Fill and Generative Expand in Photoshop driving usage growth
  - Firefly Services launched for enterprise API-driven content generation at scale
  - Express for Enterprise gaining traction with marketing teams
  - New pricing plans for Creative Cloud introduced with AI premium features

Document Cloud:
  - Document Cloud ARR: $3.21 billion (end of Q2 FY2024)
  - Acrobat AI Assistant general availability announced, early adoption strong
  - PDF collaboration features seeing increased enterprise usage
  - Reader mobile app user base growing, driven by AI summarization features

==========================================================
DIGITAL EXPERIENCE — Q2 PERFORMANCE
==========================================================

Digital Experience segment revenue: $1.33 billion (up 9% YoY)
Digital Experience subscription revenue: $1.20 billion (up 11% YoY)

Key highlights:
  - Adobe Experience Platform and native applications driving digital transformation
  - GenStudio for Performance Marketing announced, combining content supply chain with analytics
  - Content Supply Chain solution gaining enterprise interest
  - Customer Journey Analytics adoption accelerating in retail and financial services
  - AEM as a Cloud Service migrations continuing from on-premise AEM installations

==========================================================
NOTABLE RISKS IN Q2
==========================================================

  - Figma acquisition terminated — Adobe paid $1 billion breakup fee in Q1 FY2024
  - Regulatory scrutiny increasing globally — EU Digital Markets Act and AI Act creating compliance costs
  - Subscription cancellation lawsuit alleging deceptive practices — potential reputational risk
  - Consumer creative tool market fragmenting with free AI alternatives reducing barrier to entry
  - SMB segment showing price sensitivity to AI-tier upselling
  - Macroeconomic uncertainty in Europe and Asia affecting enterprise deal velocity

==========================================================
CASH FLOW AND CAPITAL ALLOCATION — Q2
==========================================================

Operating Cash Flow: $2.39 billion in Q2 FY2024
Free Cash Flow: approximately $2.30 billion
Share Repurchases: $2.2 billion in Q2 FY2024
Cash and Short-Term Investments: $7.69 billion (end of Q2)
Remaining Performance Obligations: $17.82 billion

==========================================================
MANAGEMENT OUTLOOK — Q2
==========================================================

  - Full-year FY2024 revenue target: approximately $21.40–$21.50 billion
  - Non-GAAP EPS target for FY2024: approximately $18.00–$18.20
  - Management highlighted AI monetization as multi-year revenue catalyst
  - Firefly commercial model validated — consumption-based pricing scaling
  - International expansion in Japan and India highlighted as growth vectors
